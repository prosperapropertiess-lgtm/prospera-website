# Property Management in St. Thomas, Ontario: What Landlords Need to Know

St. Thomas is quietly becoming one of Southwestern Ontario's most attractive rental markets. With Amazon's massive fulfillment centre bringing thousands of jobs to Elgin County, a growing downtown core, and housing prices still far below neighbouring London, more investors are buying rental properties here than ever before.

But owning a rental in St. Thomas isn't quite the same as owning one in a big city. Local landlords face a unique mix of opportunities and challenges — from finding reliable tenants in a rapidly shifting labour market to navigating Ontario's Residential Tenancies Act without a real estate lawyer on speed dial.

This guide breaks down what property management in St. Thomas actually looks like, what to expect from a property management company, and how to decide if professional management is right for your rental.

## Why St. Thomas Is Attracting Rental Investors

The City of St. Thomas has seen significant economic investment over the past few years. The Amazon logistics campus alone created thousands of full-time positions, drawing workers from across Elgin County and beyond. Combined with ongoing infrastructure improvements and a lower cost of entry compared to London, St. Thomas has become a compelling option for both first-time and seasoned rental investors.

Rental demand is strong, vacancy rates are healthy, and average rents have climbed steadily. For landlords, that's a good news story — but it also means more competition for quality tenants and higher stakes when something goes wrong.

## What a Property Manager Does in St. Thomas

A local property management company handles the day-to-day work of being a landlord so you don't have to. In practice, that typically includes:

- **Tenant sourcing and screening** — listing your unit, fielding applications, running credit and reference checks, and selecting a qualified tenant
- **Lease preparation** — drafting a legally compliant Ontario Standard Lease with any required addenda
- **Rent collection** — collecting monthly rent, issuing receipts, and following up on late payments
- **Maintenance coordination** — fielding tenant repair requests and dispatching vetted tradespeople
- **Move-in and move-out inspections** — documenting the property condition to protect your damage deposit rights
- **Ontario LTB compliance** — filing N4 notices for non-payment, N5 notices for tenant misconduct, and representing landlords at Landlord and Tenant Board hearings when required

For out-of-town investors or landlords who simply don't want to be on call, professional management turns a hands-on obligation into a largely passive income stream.

## The St. Thomas Rental Market: What You're Working With

### Typical Property Types

St. Thomas rental stock is dominated by single-family homes, duplexes, and small multi-unit buildings. Unlike London, you'll find fewer high-rise apartments and more older housing stock — think 1960s to 1990s builds that may require more ongoing maintenance attention.

### Rent Ranges

As of 2026, average rents in St. Thomas generally run below London prices, making the city attractive for tenants priced out of bigger urban centres. A well-maintained two-bedroom unit in a quiet neighbourhood can command competitive rents while still delivering strong yields relative to acquisition costs.

### Tenant Pool

St. Thomas attracts a mix of long-term local residents, young families, and workers relocating for employment. The Amazon effect has increased demand for affordable, functional housing near major employers and transit routes.

## Common Challenges for St. Thomas Landlords

### Maintenance on Older Stock

Many rental properties in St. Thomas are older homes that require more upkeep than newer builds. Furnaces, roofing, plumbing, and electrical systems all need regular attention. Without a network of reliable tradespeople, landlords can find themselves overpaying for emergency repairs or waiting too long to address issues — which can create legal exposure under Ontario's maintenance obligations.

### Tenant Turnover

High-turnover tenants are a reality in any market with a transient employment base. Each turnover costs money: cleaning, repainting, minor repairs, advertising, and lost rent during vacancy. A good property manager minimizes turnover by screening carefully up front and handling tenant concerns promptly before small issues become reasons to leave.

### LTB Timelines

If you ever need to file with the Landlord and Tenant Board — whether for unpaid rent or persistent misconduct — be prepared for delays. Ontario's LTB has faced significant backlogs in recent years, and even straightforward cases can take several months to resolve. Proper documentation from day one (signed inspection reports, rent receipts, written notices) is essential.

## What to Look for in a St. Thomas Property Management Company

Not all property managers are created equal. When evaluating companies, ask these questions:

- **Are they familiar with Elgin County and St. Thomas specifically?** Local market knowledge matters for pricing and tenant sourcing.
- **How do they handle maintenance requests?** Do they have trusted local contractors or are they winging it?
- **What does their tenant screening process look like?** Do they verify income, check credit, and call previous landlords?
- **How do they handle LTB filings?** Can they prepare and serve proper notices themselves, or will you need a paralegal for everything?
- **What are their fees?** Most property managers charge a percentage of monthly rent (typically 8–12%) plus fees for tenant placement and other services. Make sure you understand what's included.

## Self-Managing vs. Hiring a Property Manager

Self-managing your St. Thomas rental can work well if you live nearby, have reliable trades contacts, and are comfortable with Ontario landlord-tenant law. But for investors who live out of the area, have multiple properties, or simply value their time, the math often favours professional management.

Consider this: one bad tenant, one missed maintenance issue that becomes an insurance claim, or one improperly served LTB notice can cost thousands of dollars and months of stress. A good property manager earns their fee by keeping those problems from happening in the first place.

### Does Ontario rent control apply to rental properties in St. Thomas?
Yes. All Ontario residential tenancies fall under the Residential Tenancies Act. Rent control applies to units first occupied before November 15, 2018, with the 2026 guideline set at 2.5%. Newer units are exempt from the guideline cap, but the 90-day written notice requirement and the 12-month rule between increases still apply.
